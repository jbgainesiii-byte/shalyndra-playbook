Stock Market 101: The Need-to-Know

Stock: A piece of ownership in a company.

ETF: A basket of stocks. Instead of buying one, you buy a collection (like a tech-focused or AI-focused basket).

Brokerage: The app or website where you buy and sell stocks/ETFs. Think of it as the Amazon for your investments.

Your Money Engine: The Shortcut

Stop the analysis paralysis. Use a commission-free brokerage app like Fidelity, Charles Schwab, or Vanguard.

They are reputable, easy to use, and have no fees for buying and selling most stocks and ETFs.

Your first move: Open an account and search for an AI-focused ETF. A popular one is BOTZ (Global X Robotics & AI ETF).

Real Talk: Let's Address the Fear

"I don't have enough money."

False. You can buy fractional shares for as little as $5. Starting small beats not starting at all.

"What if I lose it all?"

By investing in an ETF (a basket of stocks), you reduce risk. The market dips, but historically always trends upward long-term.
