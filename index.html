Stock Market 101 — Quick & Clear

What is it?

  • Stock = tiny slice of a company.
  • ETF = basket of many stocks (less guesswork).
  • Index = benchmark list (S&P 500, Nasdaq-100).
  • Fees (expense ratios): VOO/VTI ≈ 0.03%/yr.
  • DRIP: auto-reinvest dividends to buy more shares.

’90s Internet → Today’s AI

Real Talk — Questions You’re Probably Thinking

“I don’t have a lot to start.”

You don’t need a lot. Start with $25–$50/mo and turn on auto-deposit. Habit > amount.

“Isn’t this gambling?”

Single stocks can be. Broad ETFs (VOO/VTI) are diversified ownership of hundreds to thousands of companies.

“What if the market crashes?”

It will dip. Historically it recovers. Keep buying on a schedule—downturns become discounts.

“I’m 53—too late?”

No. 10–20 years of compounding is meaningful, especially with monthly contributions.

“Which first—debt or investing?”

Pay minimums; kill high-interest debt ASAP. You can still invest small amounts to build the habit.

“How do I pick?”

Start with one core ETF (VOO or VTI). Add QQQ/BOTZ only if you’re okay with more swings.

“Do I need to watch it daily?”

Nope. Set monthly auto-buy and check once a month. Your time is better spent on your MBA.

“What’s the risk?”

Short-term volatility. Manage it with diversification (VOO/VTI) + time in the market.

“What about taxes?”

In a brokerage, you’ll get a 1099 for dividends/realized gains. Long-term holding keeps taxes simpler.
