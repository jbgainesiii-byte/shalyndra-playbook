Stock Market 101 — Quick & Clear

What is it?

  • Stock = tiny slice of a company.
  • ETF = basket of many stocks (less guesswork).
  • Index = S&P 500, Nasdaq-100, etc.
  • Fees (expense ratios): VOO/VTI ≈ 0.03%/yr.
  • DRIP auto-reinvests dividends.

Real Talk — 9 Concerns, Straight Answers

“I don’t have much.”

Start with $25–$50/mo. Habit beats amount.

“Isn’t it gambling?”

Single stocks can be. Broad ETFs (VOO/VTI) are diversified.

“What if it crashes?”

Markets dip. Historically they recover. Auto-buy turns dips into discounts.

“I’m 53—too late?”

No. 10–20 years of compounding still matters.

“Debt vs investing?”

Pay high-interest debt fast; still invest small to build the habit.

“Which ETF first?”

Start VOO or VTI. Add QQQ/BOTZ only if you accept bigger swings.

“Do I monitor daily?”

No. Automate and check monthly—focus on your MBA.

“What’s the risk?”

Short-term volatility. Mitigate with diversification + time.

“Taxes?”

Brokerage = 1099 for dividends/realized gains. Long-term holding is simpler.
