Stock Market 101 — Super Simple

What is it?

  • Stock = tiny piece of a company.
  • ETF = a basket of many stocks.
  • Index = a list, like S&P 500.
  • Fees: VOO/VTI ≈ 0.03%/yr (very low).
  • DRIP: dividends buy more for you.

Real Talk — Simple Answers

“I don’t have much money.”

Start small. $25–$50 a month is fine. The habit matters most.

“Is this gambling?”

Pick broad funds (VOO/VTI). That spreads risk across many companies.

“What if it drops?”

It will go up and down. Keep buying on schedule. Over time, it grows.

“Am I too late at 53?”

No. 10–20 years is plenty for growth, especially with monthly deposits.

“Debt first or investing?”

Pay high-interest debt fast. Still invest a little to build the habit.

“What should I buy?”

Start with VOO or VTI. Add QQQ/BOTZ only if you’re okay with bigger swings.

“Do I watch it every day?”

No. Set auto-buy. Check once a month. Focus on school and life.

“What’s the risk?”

Short-term ups and downs. Time + diversification lower the stress.

“What about taxes?”

In a brokerage, you’ll get a 1099. Holding long-term keeps it simpler.
